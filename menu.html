Bruscceta e Crostini

Bruscetta is a big slice of Tuscan bread toasted and rubbed on both sides with garlic and doused with freshly pressed olive oil on both sides. Crostini is smaller, toasted (or fried) bread topped with liver paste, or walnuts with anchovies and butter, or stewed tomatoes and mozzarella.

- Wines -

Chianti

The vines are situated on well-exposed hills, trained prevalently by the Guyot and Cordon systems, on medium density soil of Tufo and Alberese, the two typical soils in the Chianti area of Tuscany. The grapes are picked between September and October, in the correct state of ripeness. These are then vinified using the red wine method, with careful checking of temperature and quality of maceration. The wine is aged for a number of months in tank before being bottled.

Morrellino Di Scansano

At the southern end of Tuscany, the Sangiovese grape takes on a different name: Morellino. Situated near the town of Scansano, the Morellino di Scansano DOCG produces a powerful and robust grape variety, producing a bold and full-flavored wine. Powerful enough to rival the robust wines of Piedmont, Morellino di Scansano is spicy, somewhat peppery, a full-bodied wine to accompany rich, savory meals.
